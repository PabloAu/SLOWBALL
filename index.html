What do we know about investing?

Financial markets have been studied for more than a century by academic researchers, institutional investors, and market practitioners.

The most robust insights are public, replicated, and clearly documented.

The few things that matter

  • Markets are unpredictable in the short term.

    Forecasts sometimes work, but consistent prediction is extremely rare.

  • Diversification is the reliable alternative.

    You do not depend on one company, country, sector, or event.

  • Most professionals do not outperform the market after costs.

    Avoid paying for complexity that does not reliably add value.

  • The right portfolio is personal.

    It depends on your goals, risk capacity, and time horizon — and can be understood and planned.

Why not to delegate your wealth blindly?

Because today the tools exist: low-cost ETFs, transparent brokers, and global diversification.

The principles are simple and they work over decades.
